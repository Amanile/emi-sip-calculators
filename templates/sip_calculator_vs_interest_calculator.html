Understanding SIP vs Interest Calculator

What is SIP?

Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) allows you to invest a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds. It offers the benefit of rupee cost averaging and compounding over time, making it suitable for long-term wealth creation.

Fixed Deposits vs SIP

Fixed Deposits provide guaranteed returns with capital protection, while SIPs offer potentially higher returns but come with market risks. FDs are ideal for conservative investors, while SIPs suit those seeking growth.

Risk vs Return

SIPs generally offer higher potential returns but involve market risk. Fixed deposits provide stable, predictable returns with virtually no risk to principal. Your choice should align with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

Tax Implications

SIP returns are subject to capital gains tax based on holding period, while FD interest is taxed as per your income tax slab. Consider tax efficiency when comparing investment options for your financial planning.
